Biography

My name is Nwangwa Iheanyi Emmanuel, a recent graduate of the University of Ibadan, where I majored in Educational Management and minored in Communication. Growing up in a humble home with my single mother, a hardworking trader, I witnessed the challenges of limited access to quality education. These experiences shaped my passion for bridging educational disparities, especially in underdeveloped communities where opportunities are scarce. I believe education is a transformative tool that can unlock potential and drive societal progress. My growing interest in technology, particularly ed-tech, has inspired me to focus on creating accessible, user-friendly platforms to make education available to all. To achieve this, I am pursuing the Software Engineering program at AltSchool Africa, specializing in Front-End development. My goal is to combine my educational background with technical skills to design impactful solutions that empower learners in underserved regions, contributing to a more equitable and inclusive educational landscape.

Why I Joined Alt School

I joined Alt School to gain hands-on experience, learn industry-relevant skills, and connect with a community of like-minded individuals who are passionate about technology.

My Goals

My primary goals for this program are to master software engineering concepts, build scalable applications, and contribute to impactful projects that solve real-world problems.
